Divergence Operator¶

When solving PDEs using the finite volume approach, inner products may contain the divergence operator. Where \(\psi\) is a scalar quantity and \(\vec{w}\) is a vector quantity, we may need to derive a discrete approximation to the following inner product:

\[(\psi , \nabla \cdot \vec{w}) = \int_\Omega \, \psi \, \nabla \cdot \vec{w} \, dv\]

In this section, we demonstrate how to go from the inner product to the discrete approximation. In doing so, we must construct discrete differential operators, inner product matricies and consider boundary conditions.

Import Packages¶

from discretize.utils import sdiag
from discretize import TensorMesh
import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

Background Theory¶

For the inner product between a scalar (\(\psi\)) and the divergence of a vector (\(\vec{w}\)), there are two options for where the variables should live.

For \(\boldsymbol{\psi}\) at cell centers and \(\boldsymbol{w}\) on cell faces:

\[\int_\Omega \psi \; (\nabla \cdot \vec{w}) \, dv \approx \boldsymbol{\psi^T M_c D \, w}\]

where

  • \(\boldsymbol{D}\) is the discrete divergence operator from faces to cell centers

  • \(\boldsymbol{M_c}\) is the basic inner product matrix for scalars at cell centers

For \(\boldsymbol{\psi}\) on the nodes and \(\boldsymbol{w}\) on cell edges , the divergence operator would have to map from edges to nodes. For boundary nodes, this would require knowledge of \(\vec{w}\) outside the boundary. To evaluate the inner product we use the identity \(\psi \nabla \cdot \vec{w} = \nabla \cdot \psi\vec{w} - \vec{w} \cdot \nabla \psi\) and apply the divergence theorem such that:

\[\int_\Omega \psi \; (\nabla \cdot \vec{w}) \, dv = - \int_\Omega \vec{w} \cdot \nabla \psi \, dv + \oint_{\partial \Omega} \psi (\hat{n} \cdot \vec{w}) \, da \approx - \boldsymbol{\psi^T G^T M_e \, w } + \boldsymbol{\psi^T B \, w } = \boldsymbol{\psi^T \tilde{D} \, w }\]

where

  • \(\boldsymbol{G}\) is the discrete gradient operator from nodes to edges

  • \(\boldsymbol{M_e}\) is the basic inner product matrix for vectors on edges

  • \(\boldsymbol{B}\) is a sparse matrix that imposes boundary conditions on \(\vec{w}\)

  • \(\boldsymbol{\tilde{D}} = \boldsymbol{- G^T M_e + B}\) acts as a modified divergence operator with boundary conditions imposed

Note that when \(\hat{n} \cdot \vec{w} = 0\) on the boundary, the term containing \(\boldsymbol{B}\) is zero.

Divergence¶

# Make basic mesh
h = np.ones(10)
mesh = TensorMesh([h, h, h])

# Items required to perform psi.T*(Mc*D*v)
Mc = sdiag(mesh.vol)  # Basic inner product matrix (centers)
D = mesh.faceDiv  # Faces to centers divergence

# Plot sparse representation
fig = plt.figure(figsize=(8, 5))

ax1 = fig.add_subplot(111)
ax1.spy(Mc * D, markersize=0.5)
ax1.set_title("Mc*D", pad=20)
